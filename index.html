Featured Work

Kyomen

A fast-paced bullet hell with light roguelike elements where players equip two masks from a pool of six, each defining unique abilities and playstyles. Set on a collapsing 5x5 grid, creating constant spatial pressure as tiles fall away and enemies spawn, forcing reactive moments and decision-making.

Contributions: Collaborative systems and gameplay design, enemy/AI design. Full model texturing, learning and applying Substance Painter to establish a consistent visual identity across the project.

What I Learned: Developed strong skills in material creation and visual identity, alongside rapid team-based iteration through continuous playtesting.

Honours Project - Adaptive AI NPC Study

A solo Unreal Engine research prototype created to investigate how scripted and adaptive reinforcement learning-driven NPC behaviours affect player engagement and perceived fairness in a controlled 1v1 scenario.

Contributions: Solo development, AI behaviour design, gameplay loop design, in-game survey flow, CSV data export, participant testing and data analysis.

What I Learned: Developed a stronger understanding of controlled playtesting, ethical participant research, adaptive AI design, questionnaire design and how player perception can differ from intended system behaviour.

Play / Page

AI Movement Prototype

An Unreal Engine prototype exploring AI movement behaviour and readable NPC reactions. The prototype contains three AI characters that share a single AI controller structure, with different behaviour driven through Behaviour Trees: one follows the player, one flees from the player and one attempts to maintain distance by repositioning when interrupted.

Contributions: Designed and built the prototype independently, including the AI controller setup, Behaviour Tree logic, sensing behaviour, movement responses and simple presentation flow for testing.

What I Learned: Gained practical experience creating AI behaviour from scratch in Unreal Engine, using Behaviour Trees to organise logic, and understanding how small changes in movement rules can make NPC behaviour feel more reactive and readable.

Projects

Just a Chill Fishing Game

A simple, goal-driven fishing game built around a relaxing, light progression loop. Players are tasked with catching specific fish by casting a hook and guiding it toward targets, while avoiding bubbles that can knock fish away or disrupt the line.

Contributions: Gameplay and systems design within a team of five during a 48-hour Global Game Jam. Focused on skill tree design and balancing, alongside contributing heavily to making pixel art for the game.

What I Learned: Learned to work effectively under tight time constraints, contributing to a complete and functional game within 48 hours. Improved collaboration under pressure and gained experience prioritising features to maintain a clear and polished core gameplay loop.

Sneaky Saving

An educational stealth/avoidance game created to teach students living in accommodation about energy-saving habits. Players control an energy guardian moving through homes, completing energy-saving tasks while avoiding detection by residents.

Contributions: Worked across the majority of the design work, including level design, systems, gameplay mechanics, win/loss conditions, sound sourcing and research into energy-saving behaviours. I also supported another designer by helping explain design principles around level structure and player experience.

What I Learned: Gained a stronger understanding of designing around a client brief, especially when turning a real-world educational topic into playable mechanics. The project also showed how games can communicate practical information through interaction rather than passive instruction.

Experimental Bike Prototype

A 2D experimental platformer built around physics-driven movement and player-created terrain. The player controls a bike using hinge joint physics, navigating the level by drawing their own paths with a limited ink resource.

Contributions: Designed and developed the prototype independently, focusing on the core mechanics including physics-based bike movement, hinge joint behaviour and the real-time terrain drawing system.

What I Learned: Explored how input methods influence gameplay, particularly how drawing tablet interaction can create a more fluid and intuitive experience. Also gained experience working with physics systems and designing mechanics around player-created solutions rather than predefined paths.

Tower of Tales

A bullet hell boss arena game inspired by Arena of the Mad King, The Binding of Isaac and Realm of the Mad God. Players face a single boss encounter, dodging projectile patterns while looking for opportunities to deal damage in a fast-paced arena setting.

Contributions: Worked on overall design, focusing on boss design, attack patterns, character design and a small amount of narrative.

What I Learned: This was my first full project, where I developed a foundation in game design principles such as pacing, game feel and readability. It also reinforced the importance of simplicity in design, focusing on making core mechanics work well rather than overcomplicating systems.
